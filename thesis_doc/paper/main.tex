Vision--language models now answer questions about documents, charts, and natural images by aligning a frozen vision encoder with a pretrained language model.
For structured visual content such as charts and documents the reasoning steps are explicit, which makes both the choice of alignment objective and the trustworthiness of the model's stated reasoning especially consequential.
Prior work studies alignment and rationale faithfulness mostly through evaluation-only benchmarks or reinforcement-learning methods, and rarely under a fixed low-resource fine-tuning budget.
It therefore remains unclear whether inexpensive supervised explanation-aware fine-tuning improves not only answer accuracy but the faithfulness of generated rationales, and whether any such benefit depends on the visual domain.
We fine-tune open-source vision--language models with QLoRA under four alignment variants---baseline, contrastive-enhanced, generative, and explanation-aware generative---holding data and budget fixed so that only the training signal varies.
We evaluate on DocVQA, ChartQA, ScienceQA, A-OKVQA, and VQAv2 along answer accuracy, explanation quality (BLEU, ROUGE-L, BERTScore), and two faithfulness measures (attention-alignment and evidence-masking consistency).
